Synopsis

Direct Study program was conducted in the summer of 2024 under the supervision of Prof. Thierry Laurens. The primary reference we employed was the second edition of Partial Differential Equations by Lawrence C. Evans. Over a period slightly exceeding three months, we commenced our studies with four fundamental types of equations: the transport equation, the Laplace equation, the heat equation, and the wave equation. Subsequently, we elected to delve deeper into the wave equation and certain dispersive equations featured in this tome. We thoroughly covered all chapters pertinent to the wave equation, with particular emphasis on the newly introduced chapter on nonlinear wave equations in the second edition of Evans' textbook. We judiciously omitted chapters less germane to our objectives. Where necessary, Professor Thierry Laurens furnished me with exercise problems to bolster my comprehension. In summation, over the course of this slightly more than three-month period, I have theoretically acquired a proficient toolkit for solving wave equations.
