Weak KAM Theory

Details

Duration: August-September
Material 1: Weak KAM Theory and Partial Differential Equations, Lawrence C. Evans
Material 2: A course on weak KAM theory, Hung Vinh Tran, Yifeng Yu.

Prelude

During this month, we were actually approaching the end of the Graduate Partial Differential Equations reading course. However, due to Professor Thierry Laurens' absence from campus for conferences and other commitments, we were unable to begin the next phase. As a result, I decided to explore and study more about nonlinear PDEs on my own (I believe learning more is always beneficial). Eventually, I found a topic that intrigued me which is the Weak KAM theory and PDEs. I am also very grateful to Professor Thierry Laurens, and after he returned to campus, I consulted him about some of the questions that arose during my reading over the past month.

Synopsis

First I started from the material 2 by learning the Legendre transform (Chpater 1), then moved to material 1 and studied the Weak KAM theory from the perspective of PDE based on this material. This included some aspects of dynamical systems, Calculus of variations, and Hamiltonian dynamics. My focus was on the Lagrangian approach to Weak KAM theory and the nonlinear PDE methods, particularly the application of the Lax–Oleinik semigroup, the Weak KAM theorem, viscosity solutions, Mather set and its relation to Hamiltonian dynamics, and the further analysis of important properties of Weak KAM theory through PDE methods.
